The Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel) is more than just a marketplace — it's the beating heart of Tel Aviv. Just steps from House of Delray, this bustling corridor of flavors, colors, and sounds has been the city's main market since the 1920s.

## When to Go

The market is open Sunday through Friday, but the best time to visit is Friday morning before Shabbat. That's when the energy peaks — vendors call out deals, locals stock up for the weekend, and the air is thick with the scent of fresh spices and baked goods.

## What to Eat

Start with a fresh-squeezed pomegranate juice at one of the stands near the Allenby Street entrance. Work your way through sabich, halva by the slice, and finish with a Turkish coffee from one of the old-school stalls.
